2. ‚úä Freedom Struggle (1885‚Äì1947)

The Indian freedom struggle was the long and powerful movement by the people of India to gain independence from British rule. It began with the First War of Independence in 1857, also called the Sepoy Mutiny or Revolt of 1857. Although this first revolt failed, it inspired future generations to continue fighting for freedom.

Over time, many leaders, movements, and ordinary people joined the struggle. The Indian National Congress was formed in 1885 to give Indians a voice in the British government. Later, leaders like Bal Gangadhar Tilak, Lala Lajpat Rai, and Bipin Chandra Pal demanded full independence. In the early 1900s, a new wave of revolutionaries like Bhagat Singh, Chandrashekhar Azad, and others used bold actions to challenge British rule.

After many years of sacrifice, struggle, and unity, India became free on 15th August 1947. The freedom struggle was not just about fighting the British it was about bringing people together, breaking social divisions, and building a nation.
